Indonesia GIS FRM Roadmap Project

Asian Development Bank Technical Assistance Project TA 9634-REG: Strengthening Integrated Flood Risk Management - National Flood Risk Management (FRM) Roadmap and investment program for Indonesia

The Flood Risk Management (FRM) Roadmap was developed by considering the FRM interventions identified at the Balai (river basin organizations) level in the context of flood risk issues and the set of national priorities in Indonesia. The interventions developed by the Balai were presented in their Rencana (a 20-year masterplan) documents. Interventions relevant to flood-related activities were compiled into a GIS database. The database included more than 8000 flood-related interventions flooding across 35 Rencana documents.
A flood management atlas was created to map flood-risk-related parameters and the interventions proposed for each Balai. Those parameters included land surface elevation, flooded area for 100-year and 500-year return period event for undefended cases (based on JBA flood maps), agricultural land exposed to 100-year return period fluvial flooding, land cover, population density, the proportion of people living in poverty, and Rencana interventions.

This FRM roadmap was then integrated with the NIRA (National Integrated Risk Analytics) tool in order to create a framework to prioritize flood risk investments in Indonesia. This project was presented at the EGU General Assembly 2022. The full abstract is published on its website.

Tools: QGIS, Google Earth Pro, Google Maps, Excel

In order to provide a brief overview of each River Basin Territory, a standardized summary table was prepared for the basins for which an approved Rencana is available. These summary tables contain compiled information on: institutional setting; basin climate and geography; flood hazard characteristics, flood exposure characteristics, flood vulnerability situation; flood damages from notable historical events, summary climate change information, land use concerning urban, agriculture and forest; information on environment and NBS (Nature-Based Solution); summary list of proposed project in the Rencana based on category types; financing requirements for the proposed interventions; and some selected major ongoing flood projects.
